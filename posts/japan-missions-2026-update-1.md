## Thoughts on Missions 💭

For the past 10 years in Austin, I've gotten the opportunity to go on several missions projects (Detroit ×2 and Peru). Through it, my conviction was to stay in the states in ATX and be a **global sender** with a **local missional mindset**. ✈️⛪

**Global sender** — Serving in my local church, I never felt called to any specific people group or country. Instead, I enjoyed supporting others through my time, talents, and resources. My kingdom impact was better helping others be sent to other places than for me to be sent out.

**Local missional mindset** — I felt convicted to learn how to live missionally where I was placed. Over the years, I learned that missions wasn't a special privilege only for missionaries who sold everything and flew out to a remote country. It was an intentional mindset to live out daily, wherever you are.

Near the end of 2024, I started feeling myself open up to being involved in missions differently. A few key things happened:

**2025 goal to explore missions** — Our church had a mission trip to Nepal + Japan in summer 2025. I was funemployed when it came out and decided not to go, but I wrote "Explore Japan missions + connections" in my 2024 yearly reflection and 2025 goals doc.

**Traveling + meeting diverse people + "what ifs"** — Through trading card games, I got the opportunity to travel and meet a lot of people from across the world. I learned about working with people from different cultural and ethnic backgrounds, understanding their viewpoints, and connecting through shared experiences. And as I visited a new city, state, or country, I'd give myself the freedom to explore the thought of — *what if I moved here? Could I thrive?*

**Multilingual capabilities unlocked** — Through travel, I found I could survive and thrive speaking Chinese in Taiwan. During job searching, I interviewed for bilingual positions and realized I could potentially work speaking two languages. And through Flesh and Blood, being able to speak two languages opened up opportunities to travel and work in HK.

## Japan Missions, Feb 2026 🗾

When our church shared this mission trip for Japan in February, I felt a tug to join — but also wrestled with a lot of questions:

- How can I go on missions in Asia when I've always seen it as a place for vacation or time with family? 🌴
- Will time off be approved, and how will I plan around future things? 📆
- Is there another opportunity to wait and explore missions more? 🔭
- Will I hit my financial support goal? 💸

In the end, I surrendered all these questions to God and trusted that in joining, He would reveal answers throughout the process. And He has:

- Time off was approved ✅
- I've learned about mobilization in missions 🔥
- I've gotten to hear about missions in Asia and Latin America 🤓
- I am fully funded 🥳

I'm still learning a lot and still have many questions — but I'm grateful for how God has answered and exceeded my expectations. Looking forward to more that He continues to reveal through our training, missional work, and post-reflections.
