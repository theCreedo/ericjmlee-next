## Mindset Going Into Missions 🧠

Going into Japan, I had a lot of questions swirling around:

- What does missions look like in Asia?
- Will Japan be hard for missions?
- How do missionaries in Japan find hope over the years when things are difficult?

But amidst all those questions, I had one clear mindset I wanted to carry in: **to encourage and to be encouraged.** 🫂

**To encourage** — knowing we were only there for 10 days, what mattered most wasn't how much we could accomplish, but how we could ease the load and encourage the missionaries in their ongoing work: through service, life sharing, prayer, and just being present.

**To be encouraged** — short-term missions would give me firsthand insight into missions in Japan — spiritually, culturally, and practically. And hearing the testimonies of those committed to the long haul would fuel my own faith and pursuit of missions.

The hope was that the time would be mutually fueling, just like how Paul felt with the church of Philippi:

> "I thank my God in all my remembrance of you, always in every prayer of mine for you all making my prayer with joy, because of your partnership in the gospel from the first day until now. And I am sure of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion at the day of Jesus Christ. It is right for me to feel this way about you all, because I hold you in my heart, for you are all partakers with me of grace." — Philippians 1:3–7b

## Personal Takeaways ✍️

I was really blessed by my takeaways from Japan. Some confirmed what I expected, some were challenging, and some gave me a lot to think about going forward.

### God is at the door 🚪

One thing that deeply moved me was seeing how God is already at work in Japan — just waiting and ready for people to come to know Him.

Japan is a country where less than 1% of the population is Christian, and missions there is genuinely hard and slow work. But what struck me was the hope that the missionaries and ministry partners carried despite that reality. They weren't discouraged by the difficulty. They were anchored in the truth that God loves the people of Japan deeply, and that He is faithful to complete the work He started there. 🙏

That hope was contagious. Being around people who chose to show up day after day, trusting that God is at the door and that revival is possible, was one of the most faith-stirring things I experienced on the trip. 🔥

### Missions in Japan was harder than I expected 😅

Going in, I honestly thought it would feel natural — being Asian, having done missions before, having visited Japan. But I was quickly humbled. 🥔

A couple things made it tough:

- Japan's culture is rooted in honor, respect, and quiet demeanor. And I am naturally loud and clumsy. 😂 Being "on" all the time and refraining from how I'd naturally express myself took a lot of energy.
- Even though I went as an attendee, my instinct to facilitate and lead was hard to switch off. Learning to submit to the leaders and surrender control was a real challenge. 🙇‍♂️
- And honestly — I don't speak Japanese. 😬

I went in thinking it would feel doable, and got quickly humbled by the reality of language and how different my personality is from Japanese culture. God was really pressing on me how much I naturally live for myself — my way of doing things, my instinct to lead, my comfort. But He was faithful in giving supernatural strength through it all, and reminding me that it's Him who establishes my steps. 🙏 (Proverbs 16:9)

### Reflecting on the cost of missions 💸

One conversation that really stuck with me was with one of our ministry partners in Toyama. He was from the states, working in Japan as an English teacher, and had been wrestling with and running away from the idea of staying long-term. But during a prayer gathering, he shared that he felt convicted to accept building his life there. Hearing him reach that conclusion in real time was really moving. 🥹

And he wasn't alone. Whether it was pastors, English teachers, or professionals who found a church and just never left — each person we met had counted the cost and concluded it was worth it to stay and serve in a country where less than 1% of the population is Christian. 🏔️

Seeing their faith and conviction made me feel like all the difficulty from the previous point was worth it. And honestly, it left me sitting with some real questions of my own: *Do I have the conviction and courage to surrender to the cost, wherever that place might be?* (Still wrestling with that one. 🤔)

### Grateful to partner with the HK team 🇭🇰

In the midst of all the stretching and humbling, one of the sweetest parts of the trip was getting to do it alongside the Hong Kong team. Spending quality time together and experiencing the DNA of a sister church in action was such a gift. Even though we're now back on opposite sides of the world, there's something really encouraging about knowing others are out there living missionally from the same network of churches. 🫂

### Falling back in love with God's Word 📖

One thing I really didn't expect was how much this trip would reignite my love for reading the Bible. Our team read through 2 Timothy together, and being able to dive deep into Scripture over several days reminded me that Bible reading is a joy, not a task to check off. It's something I want to carry back into my daily rhythm — understanding more deeply the literature and themes within the Bible. 🙂

## What's Next 🔭

After the trip, we were challenged to put together a 1-month and 1-year plan. Here's where I'm at:

🙏 **Praying over open doors** — I don't feel a specific pull back to Japan right now, but I'm staying open to wherever God might call next. Missions in another city, or being part of a church plant, is something I hold with open hands. (Especially as our church carries a vision to church-plant over the next 10 years.)

📖 **Deepening my foundation in God's Word** — I want to invest more in studying the Bible, understanding its themes and literature, and building a more solid foundation for whatever I'm called to next — whether that's missions, church planting, or everyday life.

✈️ **Mobilizing others** — For the next year, I want to be more involved in helping others take steps toward missions. Whether that's encouraging someone to explore it or helping them feel equipped to consider long-term missions. This trip was a leap of faith for me, and I hope to help others take theirs. 🫡
