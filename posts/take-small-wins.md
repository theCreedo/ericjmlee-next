_**Savvy Idea: By compounding small wins, you can gain momentum to complete goals that feel large & insurmountable.**_

Recently, I’ve been trying to flesh out a plan for a podcast, and I found myself overwhelmed with the big picture needed to be completed.

I found myself facing a lot of fears.

The fear of being imperfect.

The fear of not having all things planned out.

The fear of not doing well.

All these fears made working on the project feel insurmountable.

As I was reflecting on these fears, an enterpreneur shared to me the importance of taking small wins.

Focus on the big picture is important, but in order to not become overwhelmed, it’s important work in smaller increments.

Taking a large task or project, and breaking it into smaller chunks make it easier to digest.

By taking wins through smaller tasks, they can build up momentum in order to take on larger tasks and achieve big wins.

_Questions to ponder:_ 🤔

-   _What large project or goal do you have that feels insurmountable? What fears to do you feel with them?_
    
-   _How can you break that large project or goal into smaller tasks?_
