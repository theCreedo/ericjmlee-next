## Thoughts & Ponderings Since Last Update 💭

It's been a wild past 4 weeks — 3 weeks of fasting, a congregational retreat, hobbies, communities, and a physical challenge on top of work and life. (Big stressed 😅)

Through it all, I initially felt overwhelmed and struggled with preparing my heart for Japan while staying present to what was right in front of me. The truth is, I had a hard time just being present with God — always in that "always busy, on the go" mindset. 🏃‍♂️

But God gave me a reminder near the end of fasting and over retreat: the parable of Mary and Martha in Luke 10:38–42.

> "Now as they went on their way, Jesus entered a village. And a woman named Martha welcomed him into her house. And she had a sister called Mary, who sat at the Lord's feet and listened to his teaching. But Martha was distracted with much serving. And she went up to him and said, 'Lord, do you not care that my sister has left me to serve alone? Tell her then to help me.' But the Lord answered her, 'Martha, Martha, you are anxious and troubled about many things, but one thing is necessary. Mary has chosen the good portion, which will not be taken away from her.'"

I felt like Martha over those 4 weeks — trying to take care of all the things. But what I needed was to be like Mary: even with lots to take care of, to be reminded of the good portion I have in Jesus and choose Him. 😮

And God was good at working that reminder in. At the retreat, I had some precious time being like Mary. 🙂 During our Saturday evening session, after worship, I was able to let all the weight off my shoulders — to cry and just be in God's presence. It was such a sweet time, being reminded that nothing is too much for Him to carry.

I was able to sit in the truth and know that He knows my heart and soul wholly and fully. 🫂

After retreat, with one week left before the trip, even though things were still chaotic externally — lots of packing, last two training sessions, tying up loose ends at work 🪢 — my heart felt more at peace. Not only did I have more capacity to think about Japan, the team, and the ministry work, but I was reminded of a truth: He already knows the plans He has for us. One verse that keeps coming back to me is Proverbs 16:9:

> "The heart of man plans his way, but the LORD establishes his steps."

No matter all our plans, I trust God already knows what's to come. He has called us to this trip, each from our own unique testimony. And I believe by faith He will complete the work He has already started — not only for everyone on the team, but for those we'll work with and encounter in Japan. We just have to be open, willing, and available. 🫡
